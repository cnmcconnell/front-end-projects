Greenville, SC is a gorgeous city, nestled in the Blue Ridge mountains that provides a vast range of different restaurants, events, and shops. With breathtaking scenery and a bustling downtown, Greenville is a very popular spot for foodies, art enthusiasts, and nature loves alike. Downtown provides everything from unique shops and boutiques to museums and various outdoor activities, ensuring it’s visitors are entertained all year round. It’s award-winning Main Street has a clever little game called “mouse on main”, where visitors can read riddles and search out tiny mice statues hidden around town. Among these, the streets are scattered with various art sculptures, friendly locals, and diverse restaurants. A popular spot includes the gorgeous Falls Park where Liberty Bridge, a 345 ft long, single suspension bridge, overlooks 30-foot waterfalls and relaxing, yet lively gardens.

History

Greenville city was once a hunting ground for the Cherokees. In 1770, Richard Pearis established a plantation and later established Greenville County in 1786. The county was later used for cotton manufacturing, when Vardry McBee constructed a mill in the 1830s. It wasn’t until the 1920s that the city began to truly develop, though coming to a standstill during the Great Depression. Charles Daniel, along with others, helped attract companies and create a strong workforce in the 1950s and 60s, triggering an economic revival.
